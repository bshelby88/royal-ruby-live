## Email 02 — Day 1 — The one habit

**Subject:** Your score is being graded — randomly
**Preheader:** Utilization is 30% of your score and most people get it wrong

---

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

Did you pull your reports yet? If yes, bookmark this email for after you're done. If not — go do that first. This email waits.

Here's the thing nobody tells you about credit card utilization.

The bureaus don't wait until your statement closes. They snapshot your balance **whenever the lender reports** — and that can be any day of the month.

Which means you can be "responsible" — paying in full on the due date every single month — and still get graded at 90% utilization because the bureau happened to look right before you paid it off.

The fix is one sentence: **Pay it down *before* the statement closes. Not after.**

Some people do it twice a month. Some do it the day before statement date. Either works. The important thing is your reported balance is low, not your paid balance.

This one habit is worth twenty to forty points. For most of my clients it's the single biggest move they make in the first thirty days.

Page 2 of the checklist has the exact protocol.

## Email 03 — Day 3 — The one mistake

**Subject:** The 1-in-5 mistake on your credit report
**Preheader:** The FTC's number, not mine

---

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

The Federal Trade Commission published a study. One in five credit reports has a material error.

Not "one in five people" — one in five *reports.* Meaning: you have three reports, so statistically *at least one of them is wrong about you right now.*

The errors the FTC found:
- Accounts that don't belong to you
- Paid accounts still showing as unpaid
- Wrong balance amounts
- Duplicate tradelines
- Old addresses the bureaus never cleaned up
- Mixed files — someone with a similar name whose data leaked onto yours

Every single one of these is removable. You have a federal right under the Fair Credit Reporting Act to dispute anything inaccurate. The bureaus have 30 days to verify. If they can't verify — they *must delete.*

This is the most under-used right in personal finance. People accept the score the bureaus hand them like it's a medical diagnosis. It's not. It's a spreadsheet and spreadsheets have typos.

Page 3 of the checklist walks you through how to find the errors. Page 4 has the dispute-letter anatomy — the five things every successful letter contains.

Read it. Tonight if you can.

— Dr. Marigny

## Import instructions

### ConvertKit (recommended)

1. Go to **Automations → New Automation → Blank**
2. **Trigger:** Form subscribed → select your "Free Checklist" form
3. Add **6 Email sequences** spaced at the days above
4. For each email, paste subject, preheader, body (replace `[FIRST_NAME]` with ConvertKit's `{{ subscriber.first_name | default: "friend" }}`)
5. Activate

### Mailchimp

1. **Customer Journeys → + Create**
2. **Starting point:** Form submission
3. Add **Send email** steps with delays
4. Use merge tags `*|FNAME|*`

### Formspree → manual (current state)

Right now you don't have an email service wired. Your Formspree submissions go to your inbox. Until you add ConvertKit/Mailchimp, these emails are just drafts — save them for later. The moment you move to an ESP, paste them in.
