## Thread 04 — The utilization trick

1/ Your credit score is being graded at random moments. And most people are getting a failing grade without even knowing it.

{{MEDIA: 04-utilization.mp4}}

2/ Here's the thing about utilization:

The bureaus don't wait until your statement closes. They snapshot your balance *whenever the lender reports* — which can be any day of the month.

3/ So you can pay off your card in full on the 28th like a responsible adult, but the lender already reported your 90%-full balance on the 15th.

The bureau sees the 90%. Your score drops.

4/ The fix is timing, not spending:

Pay your card down BEFORE the statement closes. Not after.

5/ Some people do it twice a month. Some do it the day before statement date. Either works.

The key: the reported balance is low, not the paid balance.

6/ Worth 20 to 40 points for most people.

## Thread 09 — The authorized user move

1/ The quietest credit move in the playbook:

Get added as an authorized user on a family member's old card.

{{MEDIA: 18-authorized-user.mp4}}

2/ What happens:

Their history appears on your report. Their credit age becomes yours. Their utilization averages into yours.

If they have a 12-year history and a $15K limit at 5% utilization — you inherit all of that.

3/ The rules:
• Long history matters more than current limit
• They need low utilization (<10%)
• You never touch the card
• You make sure they don't close it

4/ Score can move 30 to 80 points in 60 days.
